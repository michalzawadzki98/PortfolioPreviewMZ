üöÄ Challenge

L'Or√©al Paris and Saint-Gobain organized a hackathon with the aim of developing concepts for innovative solutions to challenges in the Construction & Beauty-Tech industries. The challenge presented to our team was "How can we increase the number of consumers for the official L'Or√©al store on the largest Polish marketplace (Allegro)?"

üí¢ Research & Problem Statement

Customers purchasing beauty products online face the challenge of finding the right product that meets their specific needs. The vast array of available products can be overwhelming for buyers, and the product selection process takes a considerable amount of time. Customers buying beauty products often lack sufficient knowledge about conscious skincare, making it difficult for them to choose the right product.

The issue of dissatisfaction and difficulty in finding the desired product ranks among the top 5 most commonly mentioned problems that consumers encounter during online shopping.

The presence of a wide range of products beyond cosmetics on Allegro further complicates the beauty product shopping experience, leading consumers to prefer other platforms. All of this contributes to the fact that shopping for beauty products on the Allegro marketplace is not very popular among consumers. This, in turn, results in low sales for the official L'Or√©al store on Allegro.

Sources:

  • Conducted analysis on transcripts derived from 7 in-depth interviews previously administered by an external party with female consumers of beauty products aged 16 to 37 years old.
  • Conducting approximately 10 brief interviews with consumers of beauty products and experts in the beauty and e-commerce industry at various stages of working on this project.
  • Report 'E-commerce in Poland 2023' prepared by Gemius Polska.
  • Report 'Customer Journey 2023: Searching and Purchasing Products Online' prepared by Minds&Roses.

üí° Generating Ideas & Selection

Taking into account the business requirements and the problems defined based on research and user interviews, we proceeded to generate ideas. Each team member independently generated solution ideas using the Crazy Eights method. Subsequently, we engaged in a collaborative ideation session, we additionally utilized ChatGPT to generate more ideas.

We evaluated the best ideas based on their uniqueness and how well they addressed real user problems.

We concluded that the best idea was to create an intelligent cosmetics selection assistant. Through asking relevant questions and leveraging modern technologies such as skin scanning based on photos, it would tailor the perfect product for the consumer's needs. Consequently, it would guide them to the official L'Or√©al store on Allegro, where the consumer could purchase the recommended product.

The assistant was envisioned as a web application, accessible by navigating to a dedicated website, for example, through a link. The assistant could be integrated with the Allegro website (in case of collaboration between L'Or√©al and Allegro). It would not require the installation of an application or the registration of an account.

üéÆ Prototyping & Testing

After selecting and discussing the idea in terms of the functionalities it should include, I created user interface mockups and an interactive prototype in Figma.

Since the assistant is intended to be a simple application, and I can quickly create high-fidelity mockups, I decided not to create low-fidelity mockups. In my opinion, this streamlines the testing process and helps the user better understand the tested prototype. When creating the mockups, I based them on the style of the Allegro service, as there is the possibility of integrating this tool directly with that service.

Testing with users

We conducted prototype tests with 3 beauty product consumers and 3 beauty/e-commerce industry experts.

Each user launched the prototype on their device, sharing their screen during testing. We asked questions and carefully noted users responses and thoughts.

Key notes regarding potential improvements:

  • Lack of an easy transition to searching for the next product.
  • We should display complementary products, not just a single item.
  • A brief description of the matched product is missing on the product screen.
  • We should provide users with the option to use an advanced and quick assistant form.

üíº Business: Cost & Revenue Structure

Designing a product, we take into consideration business requirements. The created product must be profitable for investors. We have developed an approximate cost structure for creating the assistant in the MVP version.

We took into account that Loreal, as a leader in the beauty industry, already has some ready-made solutions in the field of artificial intelligence and image processing for skin analysis. We considered not only the implementation costs but also the promotion of the tool.

Approximate cost structure:

  • Software Development: ~$52,000
  • Testing: ~$11,000
  • Implementation: ~$36,000
  • Training: ~$10,000
  • Technical Support: ~$10,000
  • Contingency Reserve: ~$5,000
  • Promotion: ~$41,000"

The total cost of creating the MVP version is approximately ~$207,000.

L'Or√©al, as a leader in the beauty industry, has resources and tools that could significantly accelerate the creation of the MVP version of the assistant. In our opinion, the development of the MVP of the intelligent assistant could take approximately 6-8 months.

üí∞ Revenue Structure

How will the assistant increase the number of consumers in the official L'Or√©al store on Allegro?

The products recommended by the assistant can be purchased quickly and easily with just one click in the official L'Or√©al store on Allegro.

Implementing the assistant makes it possible to increase sales by 10-15%.

For example: if the average basket value in the L'Or√©al store on Allegro is around 100 PLN, and the store has approximately 5,000 orders per month, the assistant could increase monthly sales by the value of 50,000 - 75,000 PLN.

ü§ù Possible collaboration between L'Or√©al and Allegro

Additionally, Allegro and L'Or√©al share a common interest, as revealed in interviews with L'Or√©al experts. Allegro aims to expand the sales of beauty products on its platform. As the e-commerce leader in Poland, Allegro aligns well with L'Or√©al, the beauty industry leader.

Considering these factors, there is a genuine opportunity for collaboration between L'Or√©al and Allegro in implementing the assistant and integrating it directly into the Allegro platform.

However, if Allegro is unwilling to collaborate in this regard, the assistant can be developed as a standalone application on a dedicated domain not directly linked to Allegro. There are numerous promotion possibilities for this tool to achieve its objectives both in collaboration with Allegro and independently as part of L'Or√©al's initiatives.
